# Google Group link: "Try another account" / "You don't have permission"

The app links to your group: **https://groups.google.com/g/swim-sum-android-app-test**

If people see **"You don't have permission to access this content"** or **"Try another account"**, it’s because of the **group’s access settings**, not the URL.

## Make the group joinable

1. Open the group: [https://groups.google.com/g/swim-sum-android-app-test](https://groups.google.com/g/swim-sum-android-app-test)
2. Sign in as the **group owner/manager**.
3. Go to **Group settings** (gear icon or "Manage group").
4. Under **Access** or **Permissions**:
   - **Who can view conversations:** set to **"Anyone on the web"** or **"All members of the group"** (depending on how open you want it).
   - **Who can join the group:** set to **"Anyone on the web"** (so anyone with the link sees **Join group**) or **"Anyone can ask to join"** (they see **Ask to join** / request access).
5. Save.

After that, the **same URL** (https://groups.google.com/g/swim-sum-android-app-test) will show a **Join group** or **Ask to join** option for people who aren’t members, instead of "you don't have permission".

You don’t need a different “invite link” unless you want a special invite-only URL; fixing these settings is enough for the link in the app to work as a join link.
